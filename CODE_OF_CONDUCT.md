# Code of Conduct

This repository is maintained as a focused engineering project. Contributors and participants are expected to keep interactions respectful, direct, and technically constructive.

## Expected Behavior

- assume good intent and communicate clearly
- keep feedback specific, actionable, and tied to the code or design under discussion
- respect different levels of experience without lowering the technical bar
- prefer evidence, logs, tests, and reproducible examples over rhetoric

## Unacceptable Behavior

- harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- hostile or performative pile-ons
- intentionally disruptive behavior, spam, or trolling
- publishing private or security-sensitive information without consent

## Enforcement

The maintainer may edit, remove, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, or access that violate this policy.

## Reporting

For conduct concerns, contact the repository owner privately through GitHub rather than escalating in a public thread.
